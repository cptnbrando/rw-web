Community Activist and Organizer

When it comes to present-day issues surrounding injustice and race, the lack of the presence of the evangelical church has been discouraging to people of color and culture, while the absence of their voice has been deafening.

The May 2020 inhumane killing of George Floyd illuminated the need to take the word of God out of the box and into the streets where the need is great and crucial. Simply put, we are living in a time where fear and darkness threaten to swallow many people who feel lost, whole.

Since then, she has worked to advocate for mental health resources to be made available to marginalized communities, coordinated with churches to provide trauma counseling resources, and organized fundraisers to support the educational needs of families through the COVID-19 pandemic. Tragically, because people continue to be struck down before our eyes, she has rushed to the front lines to be a much needed resource for God’s light in spaces stricken by anger, grief and tragedy.

Leader Of People

Ms. Ralanda has a heart to encourage, strengthen and empower the next generation through education and personal awareness. For many years she has worked as a youth leader and nurtured many teens and young adults trough small groups, clubs and workshops. She has provided mindfulness education, grief therapy, personal mentorship and many specialized resources for infants and toddlers.

Ralanda is passionate about changing the live of all God’s children through intercessory prayer, imparting wisdom through of the word of God, encouraging the lost to be found, making disciples, smashing generational strongholds, empowering people and ensuring that none of us parish from lack of knowledge. The power is ours.
